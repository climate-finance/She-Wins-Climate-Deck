Financial Hygiene: Two Systems

Founders often confuse these. One keeps you out of jail, the other helps you grow.

Accounting

The Bedrock
  • Goal: Compliance, Tax, Accuracy. Looking Backward.
  • Key Feature: Segmented Accounting.
  • Critical Rule: Track Restricted Grants separate from Unrestricted Cash.

FP&A

The Architecture
  • Goal: Forecasting, Strategy, Fundraising. Looking Forward.
  • Tools: Financial Model & Cash Budget.
  • Output: Board Decks, Burn Rates, Runway Analysis.
06 / 17
FP&A Toolset

Mapping vs. Surviving

The Financial Model

"Mapping the Destination"

Use for: Fundraising & Long-term Strategy.

Best Practice: Driver-Based. Link revenue to operational drivers (e.g., site acquisition pace), not just thin air.

Integration: Must link P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow (3-Statement).

Cash Strategy Budget

"Surviving the Journey"

Use for: Weekly cash management & Survival.

Best Practice: Focus on Liquidity. Grants are "lumpy"—profit is not cash.

Safety Zone: Set alerts if cash drops below 2 months of payroll.

The Capital Stack

Building a stack beyond just equity.

Dilutive (Equity)

Most expensive capital long-term. Use for high-risk R&D and growth.

Grants

Public & Private. Free money but slow/restricted.

Bank Debt

Cheap (8-15%) but hard to get. Requires assets/profit/PGs.

Venture Debt

Fast but expensive (warrants). Only for VC-backed startups.

Alt Credit

Revenue/Grant-based. Fast, no personal guarantees (e.g., Enduring Planet).

FOAK (First-of-a-Kind)

Requires "Hybrid Capital"—mixing philanthropic grants with corporate equity to bridge the "Valley of Death".

09 / 17

The 6 Key Tradeoffs

01

Dilution

Do you give up ownership? Equity = Yes. Debt = No.

02

Cost of Capital

What is the true cost? Interest + Fees + Warrants = Effective IRR.

03

Security

What is at risk? Assets? Equipment? Your house (Personal Guarantee)?

04

Seniority

Who gets paid first in a liquidation event?

05

Time

How long until money is in the bank? Weeks vs. Months.

06

Complexity

How heavy is the diligence, legal paperwork, and reporting?

The Process

11 / 17

Running the Marathon

1

The Warm Up (Coffee Chats)

Goal: Fail safely. Pitch to friendlies to refine the story.

Treat it like stand-up comedy. Don't film the special (Tier 1 VCs) until you've practiced in the clubs.

2

The Sprint

Goal: Competitive Tension.

Target 8-12 concurrent active/real conversations. Expect 200+ convos for Seed. Do not run sequentially—you need leverage.

3

Timing Buffer

Goal: Raise Before you run out of cash.

Plan for a 25%+ buffer on both timeline and capital needs. Start 9-12 months before your zero cash date.

Building a Data Room that Closes the Round

Folder Structure

Corporate Formation docs, Cap Table
Finance Historicals + Model + Tax
IP / Tech Patents, Tech Validation
Traction MOUs, LOIs, Customer Contracts, Logos
Team Resumes, "Why Us"

Tiered Access

1. Teaser: Deck + One-pager (Open to all).

2. Data Room: Detailed docs (Only for serious diligence).

Pro-Tip: Personalization

Create copies of the data room for each investor and rename to "Data Room - [Investor Name]". It’s a small psychological trick that signals high-touch effort and partnership.

13 / 17

Navigating Diligence Like a Boss

Build Reputation

Publish insights or blogs before raising. Show up in public spaces, speak, build credibility as an expert.

Validation

Investors fear risk. Pre-empt this. Build a list of experts/references you can tap during fundraising who can validate your ideas.

The Running FAQ

Maintain a running FAQ doc. If an investor asks a question, add it. Save time for the next one.

Pitch Perfect

15 / 17

Pitch Perfect: Tailoring the Narrative

One deck to rule them all. Customize the conversation, not the PDF.

The Audience Adjustments

  • Angels Focus on "The Dream" and Vision. Sell the emotion.
  • VCs Focus on "Venture Scale" (TAM) and Unit Economics.
  • Families Focus on "Legacy". Ask: "Who is the decision maker?"
  • Impact Prove "Additionality". Would this happen without you?

The Golden Rule

"Don't maintain 5 different decks. Build one core deck with a deep appendix. Use the intro meeting to pivot the narrative based on who is in the room."

Family Office Tip

"Unlike VCs, they don't have a standard playbook. You must ask about their process early to avoid wasting time."

16 / 17

Key Takeaways

Hygiene First

Build good process (Accounting and FP&A) and establish a strong financial foundation before raising capital

Know Your Audience

Adjust your pitch and process to the investor type (Angel, VC, Fam Office, Foundation)

Evaluate Tradeoffs

Weigh Dilution against Cost, Security, Complexity, and Speed.

Run a Process

Prepare, plan, sequence, and execute on a clear and defined schedule. Don't wing it.

"Fundraising is a distraction. Build efficient systems so you can get back to work."
